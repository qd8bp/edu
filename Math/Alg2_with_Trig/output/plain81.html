  1. Question

    Consider the polynomial function below.

    f(x)=−9x2+4x−6f(x)=- 9 x^{2} + 4 x - 6

    Multiple choice: describe the end behavior.


    1. Down-down: when x→−∞x\to -\infty then f(x)→−∞f(x)\to -\infty and when x→∞x\to \infty then f(x)→−∞f(x)\to -\infty.
    2. Up-down: when x→−∞x\to -\infty then f(x)→∞f(x)\to \infty and when x→∞x\to \infty then f(x)→−∞f(x)\to -\infty.
    3. Down-up: when x→−∞x\to -\infty then f(x)→−∞f(x)\to -\infty and when x→∞x\to \infty then f(x)→∞f(x)\to \infty.
    4. Up-up: when x→−∞x\to -\infty then f(x)→∞f(x)\to \infty and when x→∞x\to \infty then f(x)→∞f(x)\to \infty.

    Solution

    The degree of the polynomial is 2, which is even. The highest-degree term’s coefficient is negative.

    Thus the end behavior is down-down: when x→−∞x\to -\infty then f(x)→−∞f(x)\to -\infty and when x→∞x\to \infty then f(x)→−∞f(x)\to -\infty.
